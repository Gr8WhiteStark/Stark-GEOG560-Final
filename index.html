Applications of GIS tools in Facilities & Space Management

Annotated Bibliography

  1. A BIM Oriented Model to a 3D Indoor GIS for Space Management - A Requirement Analysis

    S N A Syed, M., & W M N Wan, M. (2019). A BIM ORIENTED MODEL TO A 3D INDOOR GIS FOR SPACE MANAGEMENT– A REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS. IOP Conference Series.Earth and Environmental Science, 385(1)https://doi.org/10.1088/1755-1315/385/1/012046

    Annotation: The data rich environment created by Building Information Modeling (BIM) is an ideal starting place for facilities management. The authors examined the requirements of 3D GIS and created a preliminary model that integrated BIM and GIS functionalities to provide a strong tool for facilities management. Relevance to Career: This paper was exactly the kind of information I'm looking for in my day-to-day work. We have an ever expanding library of BIM data, and are looking to find ways to get the most out of it, since hosting the data and licensing the software to interact with it is expensive. Getting as much use out of our data as possible is important. Integrating GIS and BIM also provides a pathway for non-BIM users to interact with BIM data in an environment that is more user-friendly and simple.





  2. Library Space Management: A GIS Proposal

    Xia, J. (2004). Library space management: a GIS proposal. Library Hi Tech, 22(4), 375-382. https://doi.org/10.1108/07378830410570476

    Annotation: The author wanted to review current GIS solutions to examine their suitability for managing space in a library. Due to the capability of GIS tools to analyze spatial data and create interactive information, the goal of this paper was to show the process of developing, implementing, maintaining, and utilizing a GIS system for academic libraries. The conclusion was that GIS technology is an extremely effective and efficient tool for managing space in an academic library. Relevance to Career: This study provides some proof of concept for similar projects to ones like what would be run at my workplace. One of the buildings in our portfolio is a library that's been open since 1939. The article also offered a deep insight into some of the challenges of using GIS as a space management tool. My biggest takeaway was the complexity involved in creating CAD drawings to have something build from.





  3. Technological Application for the post-pandemic reopening of COVID-19 in Educational Institutions Based on Geospatial Model

    P. A. F. Arrieta and M. S. Buitrago, Technological application for the post-pandemic reopening of COVID 19 in educational institutions based on a geospatial model. 2021 Congreso Colombiano y Conferencia Internacional de Calidad de Aire y Salud Pública (CASAP), Bogota, Colombia, 2021, pp. 1-4, doi: 10.1109/CASAP54985.2021.9703378. keywords: {COVID-19;Visualization;Surveillance;Digital twin;Buildings;Process control;Human factors;Surveillance System;COVID - 19;Smart Communities;Public Health;ArcGIS Indoors.},

    Annotation: The purpose of the project this article discusses is to design an application that allows management of physical spaces of one of the facilities of the University of La Salle throughout the framework of pose-COVID-19 reopening. In order to provide adequate information about the risk levels related to reopening, an ArcGIS Indoors Application was created that allowed users to view areas where positive cases of COVID-19 had been reported, and then view the corresponding risk level by cross referencing the path taken through the facility by the positive case and themselves. Relevance to Career: Implementation of an Indoors Application has a lot of potential use cases. When multiple data types are available (CAD, BIM, GIS, etc.) indoor GIS models are an effective way to bring them together. ArcGIS Indoors also provides good methods of keeping data up-to-date as changes are made or user inputs are requested.





  4. Enhancing the TRIRIGA User Interface with GIS for Space Management Across Three Sandia Laboratory Sites

    Eshelman, L. J., & Henry, K. L. (2014). Enhancing the TRIRIGA user interface with GIS for space management across three Sandia Laboratory sites.

    Annotation: This presentation was created to demonstrate the functionality available by integrating TRIRIGA and ESRI's space management tools. The resulting design is highly available, scalable, flexible, intuitive, and provides users the ability to locate buildings, rooms, people, and environmental health and safety risks/events. Relevance to Career: TRIRIGA is an extremely versitile integrated workplace management system (IWMS). It combines the functionality of many other softwares, and builds on the effectiveness of others, like ArcGIS Indoors. I'm working on an Indoors application now that will eventually be tied in with TRIRIGA so that the maps can be utlilized my staff to interact with asset management softwares, as well as other fiscal management portions of the software like facilities condition assesment (FCA) reporting.





  5. BIM, GIS, and BI Tools For a University Asset Management System Supporting Space Management, Occupancy Evaluation, and Optimization Strategies

    Di Giuda, G. M., Meschini, S., Gasbarri, P., Accardo, D., Tagliabue, L. C., & Cacciaguerra, E. (2024). BIM, GIS and BI Tools for a university asset management system supporting space management, occupancy evaluation and optimization strategies. Journal of Information Technology in Construction, 29, 1128–1155. https://doi.org/10.36680/j.itcon.2024.050

    Annotation: Very recent work combining BIM, GIS, and Asset Management systems to manage a comprehensive portfolio at University of Turin (Italy). Work showed the combination of tools to be a suitable digital representation of the University's assets and personell data, allowing facilities administrators to make suitable planning decisions based on representative data. Relevance to Career: Multiple tools are required to create strong "digital twin" environment. The better the model, the better the decision making workflow dependent on the model. The authors also demonstrate the repeatability of the process and show a wide range of suitability beyond just the university setting.





  6. Geographic Information System Usage Options in Facility Management

    Vankova, L., Krejza, Z., Kocourkova, G., & Laciga, J. (2022). Geographic Information System Usage Options in Facility Management. Procedia Computer Science, 196, 708–716. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.procs.2021.12.067

    Annotation: This article presents a case for the use of GIS tools during the construction phase of a project with a focus on future facility management. Current trends in the construction industry in the Czech Republic point to GIS tools being largely ignored throughout the construction process. However, the tools provide so much functionality to owners, construction contractors, and project managers that the authors believe usage of the tools will only grow and expand over time. Relevance to Career: If GIS tools were implemented during construction phases of projects, there are a lot of implication for property owners. Accountability is a big one for contractors doing work on behalf of Government entities. If these tools and systems were portfolio wide and implemented at handoff from construction projects, it would provide property owners the opportunity to run further analyses and monitor facility health going forward.





  7. Development of a 3D Model of a College Camput Using ArcGIS

    Tiwari, A. V. (2025). Development of a 3D Model of a College Campus Using Arcgis. Modelling in Civil Environmental Engineering (Online), 20(1), 34–41. https://doi.org/10.2478/mmce-2025-0003

    Annotation: The authors make a case that modern urban planning and effective management of college campuses requires data in three dimensions. They present ArcGIS as a system capable of providing this data visualization. The system provides a visually appealing representation of the campus while facilitating tasks like navigating the campus and allowing stakeholders in planning procedures to understand the spatial implications of their decisions. Relevance to Career: Integrating 2D digital maps with LiDAR data to create 3D representations of as-built conditions of facilities. This allows great control and visibility for planning and operations/maintenance purposes.





  8. A New Approach to 3D Facilities Management in Buildings Using GIS and BIM Integration: A Case Study Application

    Carrasco, C. A., Lombillo, I., Sánchez-Espeso, J. M., & Balbás, F. J. (2024). A New Approach to 3D Facilities Management in Buildings Using GIS and BIM Integration: A Case Study Application. Applied Sciences, 14(23), 11097. https://doi.org/10.3390/app142311097

    Annotation: This case study examines the use of BIM and GIS data for simulating facilities maintenance management on one of the campuses of the University of Cantabira in Spain. A BIM model was created in Revit, then integrated with ArcGIS Pro to create a "Digital Twin" of the facility. The next step in creating a functioning model of a facility is creating maintenance activities the facilities team wants to monitor throughout the life of the facility. The authors concluded that the digital twin of the facility enabled the team to simulate various scenarios that may arise in their physical counterparts. Relevance to Career: Digital twins of facilities is exactly what I would like to implement with the facilities team I work in. The ability to simulate maintenance scenarios and plan accordingly would be extremely valuable for determining priority of deferred maintenance items during budgeting phases of capitol construction and renovation.





  9. A Protocol to Convert Infrastructure Data from Computer-Aided Design (CAD) to Geographic Information Systems (GIS)

    Boria, E. S., Badhrudeen, M., Fonteix, G., Derrible, S., & Siciliano, M. (2020). A Protocol to Convert Infrastructure Data from Computer-Aided Design (CAD) to Geographic Information Systems (GIS). https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2006.14112

    Annotation: The authors of this publication communicated the details of the process of converting CAD drawings to be usable in GIS programs like ArcGIS Pro. The process can be complicated, and can create issues if not done correctly. The goal of the conversion involves removing data from "data silos" and populating repositories that are accessible to all. In the case of this study, converting data from CAD to GIS also enabled the users to input the data into the asset management system, which was not possible with CAD data. The authors reccomended the following workflow: Collect data, clean data, import data into GIS, georeference the data, and then clean the newly georeference data so that any errors have been cleaned up and dealt with. Relevance to Career: This workflow was employed to convert the library of CAD drawings selected to be included in our ArcGIS Indoors model. The authors of this article are certainly correct in stating how important following this procedure properly is. Any deviation from the expected work flow creates headache at later steps by interfering with downstream processes. However, completing everything as it's suggested creates a relatively seamless experience that can be easily replicated and applied across portfolios.





  10. Intelligent Monitoring and Visualization System for High Building Nighttime Utilization Based on Image Processing

    He, Y., Yu, X., Liang, Q., Yu, P., Xie, Z., Xia, L., & Qin, M. (2024). Intelligent Monitoring and Visualization System for High Building Nighttime Utilization Based on Image Processing. Sensors (Basel, Switzerland), 24(21), 6793. https://doi.org/10.3390/s24216793

    Annotation: Rising admissions at higher education institutions in China have resulted in many colleges and universities constructing and moving into mixed-use highrise facilities, which places additional pressure on space management infrastructure. Unused space at night, particularly lab space increases risk and undermines sustainability efforts taken by these institutions. The authors were tasked with identifying nighttime usage, and presented a unique method of detection. Using security cameras, light brightness was measured and associated with building occupancy with an accuracy of 98.67%. Relevance to Career: With return to work initiatives and sustainability efforts conflicting, my employer is turning to data aquisition to determine the best compromise going forward into the new hybrid work environment. Several options for measuring occupancy have been explored, and are being tested in some of our facilities.





  11. Using Geographic Information Systems to Improve Real Estate Analysis

    Rodriguez, M., Sirmans, C. F., & Marks, A. P. (1995). Using Geographic Information Systems to Improve Real Estate Analysis. The Journal of Real Estate Research, 10(2), 163–173. https://doi.org/10.1080/10835547.1995.12090785

    Annotation: GIS provide users with the ability to improve real estate analysis. The authors used GIS tools to create a working model to help predict sale price of homes in a suburban neighborhood. Combining living area, features, and measuring the distance from a home to the central business district (CBD). This analysis concluded that GIS is an effective tool in conducting real estate research, especially over large sample sizes. Relevance to Career: Managing property from a real estate perspective is part of the role of my team. Primarily, operating as a landlord, but occasionally properties are bought and sold. Since we have an existing geodatabase that includes all of our facilities, there is potential to leverage those tools to assist in any potential market analyses in the event we need to determine fair market value for any of our properties.





  12. Ecological Security Space Management in Urban Expansion Region Based on GIS - A Case Study in Nanchong City

    Shi, T. M., Zhou, L., Li, S., & Li, K. (2011). Ecological Security Space Management in Urban Expansion Region Based on GIS - A Case Study in Nanchong City. Applied Mechanics and Materials, 71–78, 4282–4287. https://doi.org/10.4028/www.scientific.net/AMM.71-78.4282

    Annotation: The authors examined how GIS tools could be leveraged to make quantitative analyses of the impact of urban expansion and to determine the ecological security rank distribution of urban space. The study concluded: urban expansion planning needed to be implemented as soon as possible in Nanchong, China as previous chaotic expansion has strained the local ecological system, and that GIS tools greatly improved the accuracy, speed, and availability of the ecological data required to make good expansionary reccomendations. Relevance to Career: Working in an environment balancing construction, remodeling, and preserving the natural world walks a delicate line. GIS tools closing the gap between questioning and understanding the impacts of these construction projects is a huge asset to my organization.



  13. Areas of Integration of GIS Technology and Smart City Tools. Research findings

    Turek, T., & Stępniak, C. (2021). Areas of Integration of GIS Technology and Smart City Tools. Research findings. Procedia Computer Science, 192, 4681–4690. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.procs.2021.09.246

    Annotation: This study examined the crossover between GIS and Smart City management technology in major metropolises in Poland (the forty largest cities in Poland). While both sets of tools are used and there is capability for integration there, the study revealed that for most of these cities, the two toolsets are used independently of one another. Relevance to Career: Smart City management tools are becoming more ane more a part of my work. Some of the campuses of buildings under my management are large enough to be considered small cities themselves. Data collection tools like Smart City management tools are good ways to check-in with residents and visitors and to examine the condition of cities/facilities.





  14. Study on Township space management planning for rural context based on GIS technique - A case study in Shangqing Township, Anxi County

    Yuanmin Zhang, Sisheng Yang, & Weishan Wang. (2013). Study on Township space management planning for rural context based on GIS technique - A case study in Shangqing Township, Anxi County. 2013 21st International Conference on Geoinformatics, 1–5. https://doi.org/10.1109/Geoinformatics.2013.6626211

    Annotation: This article emphasizes the importance of protecting rural regional culture and adjusting course to avoid continuing the pattern of unprecedented destruction. The authors developed a system to evaluate rural context construction and assign an index to identify the risk posed to the rural community. Relevance to Career: Urban planning is an important part of any construction enterprise or property owner. Meeting the needs of the ever expanding urban environment without sacrificing the needs or culture of the rural population is a critical part of the decision making process that surrounds any expansion project. Much of the land owned by my organization will eventually be evaluated for suitability for construction and having precident to evaluate the impact on the cultural landscape of the surrounding areas is a strong step in the right direction.





  15. GIS Integrations for Campus Operations

    Henderson, D., Huang, J., & Aldonado, A. M. (2024). GIS Integrations for Campus Operations.

    Annotation: This article was an interesting presentation transcript. Three speakers presented three totally different updates on three different GIS technologies being rolled out at the University of California - San Diego. First, the construction alert system was demonstrated, then the indoors space management system, then a new campus map to replace the non-interactive existing map was presented. Relevance to Career: In meetings with the facilities team from UC, they are working very diligently toward a software stack that supports their facilities management goals as an organization. Integrations are crucial points of emphasis that work together to keep all the data sources accurate and curated automatically.
